about me

My name is Charles and I currently live in Boston MA, recently graduated from Berklee College of Music with a degree in Independent Recording and Production (IRP). In IRP I learned industry standard recording techniques for live instruments and voice. Also with a multitude of mixing courses and one on mastering, I have developed a keen ear and know many ways to make records sound good.

I am a multi-instrumentalist most proficient on the piano, but I also play drums and bass. My background in Jazz and playing instruments is a pivotal part of my identity as a musician and, regardless of what I am making, is always working in the background. Before attending Berklee I studied Jazz at a summer camp for 6 years in a row where I learned about harmony, improvisation, and how to work together with a small ensemble. I believe that Jazz is one of the purest forms of musical expression that exists and all of its elements come together even in genres besides itself to create something that is so very human that is sadly missing in a lot of music that is popular today. My passion for Jazz intersects with my love for Hip-Hop and beats. Jazz connects with Hip-Hop not only in the sounds, but the way it stirs up human interaction.

Since Fall 2025 I have been a part of small but growing Hip-Hop community in Boston called Restoring The Feeling, videos of which can be found on this site. It is primarily an open-mic/cypher event where producers can play their beats and emcees pass a mic around and freestyle. Seeing the beats I play influence flows in particular ways is super inspiring to me and always driving me to create more. For many producers such as myself, the Roland SP-404 is an integral part of the underground Hip-Hop community as it provides a platform to both create and perform beats in creative ways that other hardware pieces can't. A couple of my heroes that inspire me with the SP-404 are ohbliv and Dibia$e, who I feel like I am obligated to mention.

I love using hardware like the SP to make music, but am most proficient with Ableton Live. I have also been learning how to use FL studio recently and learned Pro Tools and Logic Pro via the aforementioned mixing and recording courses at Berklee.
